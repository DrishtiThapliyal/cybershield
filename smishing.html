📱 Smishing

🏷 Social Engineering 🟢 Beginner ⏱ 2–3 min read

What is Smishing?

Smishing (SMS Phishing) is a social engineering attack where cybercriminals use text messages to trick people into revealing sensitive information, clicking malicious links, downloading malware, or making fraudulent payments.

How Does It Work?

  1. The attacker sends a fake SMS pretending to be from a trusted organization.
  2. The message creates urgency or fear.
  3. The victim clicks a malicious link or shares personal information.
  4. The attacker steals credentials or financial information.

Real-Life Example

You receive a message saying your bank account will be blocked today unless you verify it immediately. The provided link opens a fake banking website designed to steal your login credentials.

Warning Signs

  • Urgent messages creating panic.
  • Requests for OTPs or banking details.
  • Shortened or suspicious links.
  • Unknown sender numbers.
  • Lottery or prize messages.
  • Poor grammar or spelling.

How to Stay Safe

  • Never click links from unknown SMS.
  • Verify messages through official websites.
  • Never share OTPs or passwords.
  • Block suspicious numbers.
  • Keep your phone updated.
  • Use spam filtering features.

What to Do If You Are a Victim

  • Change passwords immediately.
  • Contact your bank if financial data was shared.
  • Enable Multi-Factor Authentication.
  • Monitor account activity.
  • Report the scam.
  • Inform authorities if money is lost.

💡 Quick Tips

  • Banks never ask for OTPs through SMS.
  • Always verify before clicking.
  • Don't panic because of urgent messages.

🤔 Did You Know?

Cybercriminals prefer smishing because people often trust and respond to text messages faster than emails.
