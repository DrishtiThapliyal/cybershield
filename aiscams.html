🤖 AI Scams

🏷 Modern Threats 🟡 Intermediate ⏱ 2–3 min read

What are AI Scams?

AI Scams are cyber frauds that use Artificial Intelligence to create highly convincing fake messages, voices, images, or videos to deceive victims. Attackers use AI tools to impersonate trusted individuals or organizations and manipulate people into revealing sensitive information or transferring money.

How Does It Work?

  1. Attackers use AI to generate realistic text, voices, or images.
  2. The victim receives a convincing email, phone call, or message.
  3. The scammer pretends to be a trusted person or organization.
  4. The victim is persuaded to share confidential information or send money.

Real-Life Example

A person receives a phone call that sounds exactly like their family member asking for urgent financial help after an accident. Believing the voice is genuine, they transfer money, only to discover later that it was an AI-generated voice clone.

Warning Signs

  • Unexpected urgent requests for money.
  • Messages creating fear or panic.
  • Unusual payment methods.
  • Requests to keep conversations secret.
  • Unexpected calls from familiar voices.
  • Suspicious links or attachments.

How to Stay Safe

  • Verify requests through another trusted communication method.
  • Never share OTPs or passwords.
  • Be cautious of emotional or urgent messages.
  • Confirm identities before making payments.
  • Stay updated on emerging AI-based scams.
  • Report suspicious communications.

What to Do If You Are a Victim

  • Stop communicating with the scammer.
  • Contact your bank immediately if money was transferred.
  • Change compromised passwords.
  • Enable Multi-Factor Authentication.
  • Report the incident to cybercrime authorities.
  • Inform family members about the scam.

💡 Quick Tips

  • Always verify before trusting AI-generated content.
  • Never act under pressure.
  • Use official contact methods to confirm requests.

🤔 Did You Know?

Modern AI tools can generate realistic voices, images, and conversations within seconds, making it increasingly difficult to distinguish genuine communications from scams.
