👁️ Spyware

🏷 Common Attacks 🟡 Intermediate ⏱ 2–3 min read

What is Spyware?

Spyware is a type of malware that secretly monitors a user's activities and collects sensitive information without their knowledge. It can steal passwords, banking details, browsing history, and personal data, sending it to cybercriminals.

How Does It Work?

  1. Spyware enters a device through malicious downloads, fake apps, or phishing emails.
  2. It installs silently without the user's knowledge.
  3. It monitors activities such as keystrokes, browsing history, and login credentials.
  4. The collected information is secretly transmitted to the attacker.

Real-Life Example

A user installs a free application from an unofficial website. The app works normally, but in the background it records passwords and credit card details entered on the device and sends them to cybercriminals.

Warning Signs

  • Slow device performance.
  • Unexpected advertisements or pop-ups.
  • Browser homepage changes automatically.
  • High internet usage without explanation.
  • Unknown applications running in the background.
  • Battery draining unusually fast.

How to Stay Safe

  • Download apps only from trusted sources.
  • Keep your operating system updated.
  • Use reputable antivirus software.
  • Review app permissions before installing.
  • Avoid clicking suspicious links.
  • Regularly scan your device for malware.

What to Do If You Are a Victim

  • Disconnect the device from the internet.
  • Run a complete antivirus scan.
  • Remove suspicious applications.
  • Change passwords for important accounts.
  • Monitor bank and online account activity.
  • Restore the device from a clean backup if necessary.

💡 Quick Tips

  • Always review app permissions carefully.
  • Keep security software updated.
  • Avoid installing software from unknown websites.

🤔 Did You Know?

Some spyware can secretly activate your microphone or camera, allowing attackers to monitor victims without their knowledge.
