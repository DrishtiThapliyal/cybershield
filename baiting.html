🎁 Baiting

🏷 Social Engineering 🟢 Beginner ⏱ 2–3 min read

What is Baiting?

Baiting is a social engineering attack where cybercriminals lure victims with attractive offers such as free software, gift cards, movies, prizes, or USB drives to trick them into downloading malware or revealing sensitive information. It exploits human curiosity and greed.

How Does It Work?

  1. The attacker creates an attractive offer or leaves an infected USB drive.
  2. The victim is tempted to click, download, or connect the device.
  3. Malware gets installed or sensitive information is collected.
  4. The attacker gains access to the victim's device or accounts.

Real-Life Example

An employee finds a USB drive labeled "Confidential Salary Records" in the office parking lot. Curious, they plug it into their computer. The USB silently installs malware that steals company data.

Warning Signs

  • Offers that seem too good to be true.
  • Free premium software from unknown websites.
  • Unexpected prizes or rewards.
  • Unknown USB drives.
  • Suspicious download links.
  • Promises of exclusive content with little effort.

How to Stay Safe

  • Never plug unknown USB devices into your computer.
  • Download software only from official websites.
  • Verify offers before trusting them.
  • Use antivirus software to scan files.
  • Avoid suspicious downloads.
  • Keep your device updated.

What to Do If You Are a Victim

  • Disconnect the device from the internet.
  • Run a full malware scan.
  • Remove suspicious applications.
  • Change passwords immediately.
  • Monitor your accounts.
  • Report the incident if sensitive information was exposed.

💡 Quick Tips

  • Curiosity is often exploited by attackers.
  • Free items can hide malware.
  • Always verify before downloading.

🤔 Did You Know?

Cybercriminals intentionally leave infected USB drives in public places because many people plug them in out of curiosity.
