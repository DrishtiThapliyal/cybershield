📷 QR Code Scams

🏷 Mobile Security 🟢 Beginner ⏱ 2–3 min read

What are QR Code Scams?

QR Code Scams, also known as "Quishing," occur when cybercriminals create fake QR codes that redirect users to malicious websites, phishing pages, or fraudulent payment portals. Scanning these codes can result in stolen credentials, financial loss, or malware installation.

How Does It Work?

  1. An attacker creates a fake QR code.
  2. The code is placed on posters, payment stands, emails, or public places.
  3. The victim scans the QR code with their phone.
  4. The fake website tricks the victim into entering personal or banking information.

Real-Life Example

A customer visits a restaurant and scans a QR code placed on the table for payment. The fake QR code redirects them to a fraudulent payment page where their card details are stolen by cybercriminals.

Warning Signs

  • QR code stickers placed over existing codes.
  • Unexpected login or payment requests.
  • Suspicious website URLs after scanning.
  • Requests for sensitive personal information.
  • Poorly printed or damaged QR stickers.
  • Redirects to unfamiliar websites.

How to Stay Safe

  • Verify the website URL before entering information.
  • Avoid scanning QR codes from unknown sources.
  • Inspect QR codes for signs of tampering.
  • Use trusted QR scanner applications.
  • Never enter banking details without verification.
  • Enable Multi-Factor Authentication on important accounts.

What to Do If You Are a Victim

  • Change passwords immediately.
  • Contact your bank if payment information was shared.
  • Monitor financial transactions carefully.
  • Run a security scan on your device.
  • Report the fraudulent QR code to the organization.
  • Inform cybercrime authorities if money was lost.

💡 Quick Tips

  • Always check the destination website before proceeding.
  • Do not trust QR codes blindly.
  • Verify payment QR codes with the merchant whenever possible.

🤔 Did You Know?

Cybercriminals often place fake QR code stickers over genuine payment QR codes in public places, causing payments to be redirected to fraudulent accounts without the victim noticing.
