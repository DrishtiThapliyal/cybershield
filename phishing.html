🎣 Phishing

🏷 Common Attacks 🟢 Beginner ⏱ 2–3 min read

What is Phishing?

Phishing is a cyber attack where cybercriminals use fake emails, messages, or websites to trick people into revealing passwords, OTPs, banking details, or other sensitive information by pretending to be trusted organizations.

How Does It Work?

  1. The attacker sends a fake email or message appearing to come from a trusted source.
  2. The message creates urgency or fear.
  3. The victim clicks a malicious link or enters confidential information.
  4. The attacker steals credentials or financial information.

Real-Life Example

You receive an email claiming your bank account has been suspended and asking you to verify it immediately. The provided link opens a fake banking website designed to steal your login credentials.

Warning Signs

  • Urgent messages creating panic.
  • Requests for OTPs or banking details.
  • Suspicious or shortened links.
  • Unknown sender email addresses.
  • Poor grammar or spelling mistakes.
  • Lottery or prize messages.

How to Stay Safe

  • Never click suspicious links.
  • Verify emails through official websites.
  • Never share passwords or OTPs.
  • Enable Multi-Factor Authentication.
  • Keep devices updated.
  • Use spam filters.

What to Do If You Are a Victim

  • Change passwords immediately.
  • Contact your bank if financial data was shared.
  • Enable Multi-Factor Authentication.
  • Monitor account activity.
  • Scan your device for malware.
  • Report the scam.

💡 Quick Tips

  • Banks never ask for OTPs through email.
  • Always verify before clicking links.
  • Don't panic because of urgent messages.

🤔 Did You Know?

Phishing remains one of the most common cyber attacks worldwide because it exploits human trust rather than technical vulnerabilities.
