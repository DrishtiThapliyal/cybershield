🔒 Encryption

🏷 Data & Privacy 🟡 Intermediate ⏱ 2–3 min read

What is Encryption?

Encryption is the process of converting readable information (plaintext) into an unreadable format called ciphertext. Only someone with the correct decryption key can convert it back into its original form. It is one of the most effective ways to protect sensitive information from unauthorized access.

How Does It Work?

  1. Data is converted into encrypted form using an encryption algorithm and a secret key.
  2. The encrypted data becomes unreadable to anyone without the correct key.
  3. Authorized users use the decryption key to restore the original data.
  4. This process protects information during storage and transmission.

Real-Life Example

When you visit your bank's website using HTTPS, the information exchanged between your device and the bank is encrypted. Even if someone intercepts the data, they cannot read it without the correct decryption key.

Warning Signs

  • Websites that do not use HTTPS.
  • Browser security warnings.
  • Sharing sensitive information over unsecured networks.
  • Using outdated software with weak encryption.
  • Sending confidential files through unsecured platforms.
  • Ignoring security certificate warnings.

How to Stay Safe

  • Use websites that support HTTPS.
  • Keep devices and applications updated.
  • Use encrypted messaging platforms.
  • Enable device encryption whenever available.
  • Avoid sharing sensitive information over public Wi-Fi.
  • Protect encrypted data with strong passwords.

What to Do If You Are a Victim

  • Change passwords if confidential information may have been exposed.
  • Stop using insecure websites or applications.
  • Move sensitive data to secure platforms.
  • Enable encryption features on your devices.
  • Monitor accounts for suspicious activity.
  • Seek professional assistance if confidential data has been compromised.

💡 Quick Tips

  • Encryption protects data both while stored and while being transmitted.
  • Always look for the padlock icon and HTTPS before entering sensitive information.
  • Strong passwords improve the security of encrypted data.

🤔 Did You Know?

Every time you use online banking, secure messaging apps, or make online payments, encryption helps keep your personal and financial information safe from attackers.
