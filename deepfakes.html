🎭 Deepfakes

🏷 Modern Threats 🟡 Intermediate ⏱ 2–3 min read

What are Deepfakes?

Deepfakes are AI-generated or AI-manipulated images, videos, or audio recordings that appear to be real but are actually fake. Cybercriminals use deepfake technology to impersonate individuals, spread misinformation, commit financial fraud, or manipulate public opinion.

How Does It Work?

  1. Attackers collect photos, videos, or voice recordings of a target.
  2. AI algorithms are trained using this data.
  3. The AI generates realistic fake videos or voice recordings.
  4. The deepfake is used to deceive victims into believing it is genuine.

Real-Life Example

An employee receives a video call that appears to be from their CEO asking for an urgent fund transfer. Trusting the realistic video and voice, the employee transfers money, only to discover later that the entire conversation was AI-generated.

Warning Signs

  • Unnatural facial expressions or lip movements.
  • Audio and video that appear slightly out of sync.
  • Requests involving urgency or secrecy.
  • Poor lighting or visual inconsistencies.
  • Unexpected video or voice messages requesting money.
  • Unusual behavior from someone you know.

How to Stay Safe

  • Verify important requests through another communication channel.
  • Do not trust videos or voice messages blindly.
  • Look for visual or audio inconsistencies.
  • Stay informed about AI-generated content.
  • Confirm financial requests directly with the person involved.
  • Report suspicious media to the appropriate platform.

What to Do If You Are a Victim

  • Stop communicating with the scammer immediately.
  • Inform the person being impersonated.
  • Contact your bank if money was transferred.
  • Save evidence such as screenshots or recordings.
  • Report the incident to cybercrime authorities.
  • Warn friends and family to prevent further scams.

💡 Quick Tips

  • Seeing or hearing someone online is no longer proof of identity.
  • Always verify urgent requests independently.
  • Think before you trust AI-generated media.

🤔 Did You Know?

Advances in Artificial Intelligence have made it possible to create highly realistic fake videos and voices within minutes, making deepfakes one of the fastest-growing cybersecurity threats.
