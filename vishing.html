📞 Vishing

🏷 Social Engineering 🟢 Beginner ⏱ 2–3 min read

What is Vishing?

Vishing (Voice Phishing) is a social engineering attack where cybercriminals use phone calls to trick people into revealing passwords, OTPs, banking details, or other confidential information by pretending to be trusted organizations.

How Does It Work?

  1. The attacker calls pretending to be from a bank or government agency.
  2. They create panic or urgency.
  3. The victim is asked to share sensitive information.
  4. The attacker uses the information for fraud or account takeover.

Real-Life Example

Someone calls claiming to be from your bank and says suspicious activity has been detected. They ask for the OTP sent to your phone to "verify" your account. Once shared, your account is compromised.

Warning Signs

  • Unexpected calls requesting personal information.
  • Pressure to act immediately.
  • Requests for OTPs or PINs.
  • Threats of account suspension.
  • Caller refuses identity verification.
  • Offers that seem too good to be true.

How to Stay Safe

  • Never share OTPs over phone calls.
  • Verify the caller through official numbers.
  • Hang up and call the organization directly.
  • Don't panic under pressure.
  • Block suspicious numbers.
  • Stay informed about common scams.

What to Do If You Are a Victim

  • Change passwords immediately.
  • Contact your bank.
  • Enable Multi-Factor Authentication.
  • Monitor account activity.
  • Report the scam.
  • Block the caller.

💡 Quick Tips

  • Legitimate banks never ask for OTPs.
  • When in doubt, hang up.
  • Always verify independently.

🤔 Did You Know?

Many successful vishing attacks work because attackers exploit fear and urgency rather than technology.
