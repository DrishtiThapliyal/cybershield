🔒 Ransomware

🏷 Common Attacks 🟡 Intermediate ⏱ 2–3 min read

What is Ransomware?

Ransomware is a type of malware that locks or encrypts a victim's files and demands a ransom payment to restore access. It is one of the most dangerous cyber threats affecting individuals, businesses, and government organizations.

How Does It Work?

  1. The attacker spreads ransomware through phishing emails, malicious downloads, or infected websites.
  2. The victim unknowingly opens the infected file or link.
  3. The ransomware encrypts files and locks the system.
  4. A ransom note appears demanding payment in exchange for the decryption key.

Real-Life Example

An employee opens an email attachment that appears to be an invoice. Within minutes, all company files become encrypted and a message demands payment in cryptocurrency to recover the data.

Warning Signs

  • Files suddenly become inaccessible.
  • File names or extensions change unexpectedly.
  • A ransom note appears on the screen.
  • Unusual system slowdown.
  • Important documents cannot be opened.
  • Unknown processes running in the background.

How to Stay Safe

  • Regularly back up important files.
  • Never open suspicious email attachments.
  • Keep your operating system updated.
  • Use trusted antivirus software.
  • Avoid downloading software from unofficial sources.
  • Enable Multi-Factor Authentication whenever possible.

What to Do If You Are a Victim

  • Disconnect the infected device from the network immediately.
  • Do not pay the ransom.
  • Report the incident to cybercrime authorities.
  • Restore files using a clean backup if available.
  • Run a complete malware scan.
  • Seek professional cybersecurity assistance.

💡 Quick Tips

  • Regular backups are your best defense against ransomware.
  • Keep software and antivirus updated.
  • Think twice before opening unexpected attachments.

🤔 Did You Know?

Many organizations recover from ransomware attacks without paying the ransom because they maintain secure offline backups of their critical data.
