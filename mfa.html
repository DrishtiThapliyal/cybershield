🛡️ Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

🏷 Account & Password Security 🟢 Beginner ⏱ 2–3 min read

What is Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)?

Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) is a security method that requires users to provide two or more forms of verification before accessing an account. Even if an attacker knows your password, MFA adds an extra layer of protection that makes unauthorized access much more difficult.

How Does It Work?

  1. The user enters their username and password.
  2. The system asks for a second verification factor.
  3. The user enters an OTP, authentication app code, fingerprint, or security key.
  4. Access is granted only after successful verification.

Real-Life Example

You log into your email account using your password. Immediately after, your authentication app generates a one-time verification code. Only after entering that code can you access your account.

Warning Signs

  • Unexpected OTP messages.
  • MFA approval requests you didn't initiate.
  • Login alerts from unknown locations.
  • Someone asking for your verification code.
  • Repeated failed login notifications.
  • Suspicious sign-in approval requests.

How to Stay Safe

  • Enable MFA on all important accounts.
  • Use an authenticator app instead of SMS whenever possible.
  • Never share OTPs or verification codes.
  • Review login alerts regularly.
  • Use strong and unique passwords.
  • Store recovery codes securely.

What to Do If You Are a Victim

  • Change your password immediately.
  • Remove unknown devices from your account.
  • Update your MFA settings.
  • Review account security activity.
  • Contact the service provider if needed.
  • Monitor your account for suspicious actions.

💡 Quick Tips

  • MFA greatly improves account security.
  • Authentication apps are generally safer than SMS OTPs.
  • Never approve a login request you didn't initiate.

🤔 Did You Know?

A large number of account takeover attacks can be prevented simply by enabling Multi-Factor Authentication, even if the password has been leaked.
