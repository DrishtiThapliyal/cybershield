🪪 Identity Theft

🏷 Data & Privacy 🟢 Beginner ⏱ 2–3 min read

What is Identity Theft?

Identity Theft is a crime in which someone steals and uses another person's personal information without their permission. This information may include names, Aadhaar numbers, PAN numbers, bank details, passwords, phone numbers, or other sensitive data. Attackers use this information to commit fraud, access accounts, or obtain financial benefits.

How Does It Work?

  1. An attacker obtains personal information through phishing, data breaches, malware, social engineering, or theft.
  2. The stolen information is used to impersonate the victim.
  3. The attacker opens accounts, makes purchases, applies for loans, or gains access to existing accounts.
  4. The victim usually discovers the fraud after noticing suspicious activity or financial losses.

Real-Life Example

A cybercriminal steals a person's banking details through a phishing attack. Using the stolen information, the attacker logs into the victim's account and performs unauthorized transactions before the victim realizes what has happened.

Warning Signs

  • Unauthorized transactions or purchases.
  • Unknown bank accounts or loans in your name.
  • Unexpected password reset requests.
  • Missing emails or account notifications.
  • Login alerts from unfamiliar devices.
  • Calls regarding services you never requested.

How to Stay Safe

  • Never share personal information unnecessarily.
  • Use strong and unique passwords.
  • Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA).
  • Be cautious of phishing emails, calls, and messages.
  • Monitor bank statements regularly.
  • Protect important documents and identity records.

What to Do If You Are a Victim

  • Report the incident to the relevant authorities immediately.
  • Contact your bank or financial institution.
  • Change passwords on affected accounts.
  • Enable MFA wherever possible.
  • Monitor credit reports and financial records.
  • Inform organizations whose information may have been compromised.

💡 Quick Tips

  • Share personal information only with trusted organizations.
  • Review account activity regularly.
  • Always verify requests for sensitive information.

🤔 Did You Know?

Identity theft can continue for months before victims notice suspicious activity, making early detection and regular account monitoring extremely important.
